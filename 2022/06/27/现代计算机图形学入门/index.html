引入Homogeneous Coordinates(齐次坐标)

  • 目的是消除图像平移的特殊性，将所有变换转换成矩阵乘积形式

  • 使用方法：加入第三个维度Add a third coordinite(w-coordinate)

    • 2D point = (x,y,1)T (点)

    • 2D vector=(x,y,0)T (向量)

    • 平移：

    • $$
      \left(
      \begin{array}{1}
      x’\
      y’\
      w’
      \end{array}
      \right)

      \left(
      \begin{array}{2}

      \left(
      \begin{array}{3}
      x\
      y\
      1
      \end{array}
      \right)

      \left(
      \begin{array}{1}
      x+t_x\
      y+t_y\
      1
      \end{array}
      \right)
      $$

  • Valid operation if w-coordinate of result is 1 or 0

    • vector + vector = vector
    • point - point = vector
    • point + vector = point
    • point + point = midpoint

3D Transforms

  • Use homogeneous coordinates again

    • 3D point = (x,y,z,1)T
    • 3D vector = (x,y,z,0)T

  • In general, (x,y,z,w)(w!=0) is the 3D point (x/w,y/w,z/w)

  • Use 4*4 matrices for affine transformations

  • $$
    \left(
    \begin{array}{1}
    x’\
    y’\
    z\
    1
    \end{array}
    \right)

    \left(
    \begin{array}{2}
    a & b & c & t_x\
    d & e & f & t_y\
    g & h & i & t_z\
    0 & 0 & 0 & 1
    \end{array}
    \right)
    \cdot
    \left(
    \begin{array}{3}
    x\
    y\
    z\
    1
    \end{array}
    \right)
    $$

  • Rotation around x-, y-, or z-axis

  • $$
    R_x(\alpha)=
    \left(
    \begin{array}{1}
    1 & 0 & 0 & 0\
    0 & cos\alpha & -sin\alpha & 0\
    0 & sin\alpha & cos\alpha & 0\
    0 & 0 & 0 & 1
    \end{array}
    \right)
    $$

View/Camera Transformation

  • How to take a photo

    • Find a good place and arrange people(model transformation)
    • Find a good “angle” to put the camera(view transformation)
    • Cheese!(projection transformation)

  • Define the camera first

    • Position $\vec{e}$
    • Look-at / gaze direction $\hat{g}$
    • Up direction $\hat{t}$ (assuming perp. to look-at)

  • Key observation

    • If the camera and all objects move together, the “photo” will be the same

  • How about that we always transform the camera to

    • The origin, up at Y, look at -Z
    • And transform the objects along with the camera

  • Transform the camera by Mview

    • So it’s located at the origin, up at Y, look at -Z

  • Mview in math?

    • Translate e to origin
    • Rotates g to -Z
    • Rotates t to Y
    • Rotates (g x t) To X
    • Let’s write Mview=RviewTview 先平移再线性变换

    $$
    T_{view}=
    \left(
    \begin{array}{1}
    1 & 0 & 0 & -x_e\
    0 & 1 & 0 & -y_e\
    0 & 0 & 1 & -z_e\
    0 & 0 & 0 & 1
    \end{array}
    \right)
    $$

    • Translate e to origin

    • Rotate g to -Z, t to Y, (g $\times$ t) to X

    • Consider its inverse rotation: X to (g $\times$ t), Y to t, Z to -g

    • $R_{view}^{-1}$怎么得到的？

      • 举例：$X(1,0,0)^T\cdot R_{view}^{-1}=(\vec{g}\times\vec{t})$

    • $$
      R_{view}^{-1}=
      \left(
      \begin{array}{1}
      x_{\hat{g}\times\hat{t}} & x_t & x_{-g} & 0\
      y_{\hat{g}\times\hat{t}} & y_t & y_{-g} & 0\
      z_{\hat{g}\times\hat{t}} & z_t & z_{-g} & 0\
      0 & 0 & 0 & 1
      \end{array}
      \right)
      \rightarrow
      R_{view}=
      \left(
      \begin{array}{1}
      x_{\hat{g}\times\hat{t}} & y_{\hat{g}\times\hat{t}} & z_{\hat{g}\times\hat{t}} & 0\
      x_t & y_t & z_t & 0\
      x_{-g} & y_{-g} & z_{-g} & 0\
      0 & 0 & 0 & 1
      \end{array}
      \right)
      $$

    • 旋转矩阵是正交矩阵，所以他的逆就是他的转置。

  • Summary

    • Transform objects together with the camera
    • Until camera’s at the origin, up at Y, look at -Z

  • Also known as ModelView Transformation

Orthographic Projection

  • A simple way of understanding

    • Camera located at origin,looking at -Z, up at Y
    • Drop Z coordinate
    • Translate and scale the resulting rectangle to [-1,1]2

  • In general

    • We want to map a cuboid [l,r]$\times$[b,t]$\times$[f,n] to the "canonical(正则、规范、标准)"cube [-1,1]3

    • Slightly different orders (to the “simple way”)

      • Center cuboid by translation
      • Scale into “canonical” cube

    • Translate (center to origin) first, then scale (length/width/height to 2)

    • $$
      M_{ortho}=
      \left(
      \begin{array}{}
      \frac{2}{r-l} & 0 & 0 & 0\
      0 & \frac{2}{t-b} & 0 & 0\
      0 & 0 & \frac{2}{n-f} & 0\
      0 & 0 & 0 & 1
      \end{array}
      \right)
      \cdot
      \left(
      \begin{array}{}
      1 & 0 & 0 & -\frac{r+l}{2}\
      0 & 1 & 0 & -\frac{t+b}{2}\
      0 & 0 & 1 & -\frac{n+f}{2}\
      0 & 0 & 0 & 1
      \end{array}
      \right)
      $$

Perspective Projection

  • Most common in Computer Graphics, art, visual system

  • Further objects are smaller

  • Parallel lines not parallel; converge to single point

  • How to perspective projection

    • First “squish” the frustum into a cuboid(n->n,f->f)($M_{persp->ortho}$)
    • Do orthograpgics projection($M_{ortho}$ ,already know!)

  • In order to find a transform

    • Recall the key idea: Find the relationship between transformed points(x’,y’,z’) and the original points(x,y,z)
    • $x’ = \frac{n}{z}x$ {similar to y’}

  • In homogeneous coordinates

  • So the “squish” (persp to ortho) projection does this

    • $$
      M_{persp\rightarrow ortho}
      \left(
      \begin{array}{1}
      x\
      y\
      z\
      1
      \end{array}
      \right)=
      \left(
      \begin{array}{2}
      nx\
      ny\
      unknown\
      z
      \end{array}
      \right)
      $$

  • Already good enough to figure out part of $M_{persp\rightarrow ortho}$

    • $$
      M_{persp\rightarrow ortho}=
      \left(
      \begin{array}{}
      n & 0 & 0 & 0\
      0 & n & 0 & 0\
      ? & ? & ? & ?\
      0 & 0 & 1 & 0
      \end{array}
      \right)
      $$

    • Any information that we can user?

  • Observation: the third row is responsible for z’

    • Any point on the near plane will not change
    • Any point’s z on the far plane will not change

  • So the third row must be of the form(0 0 A B)

  • What do we know now?

  • Any point’s z on the far plane will not change

  • Solve for A and B

  • Finally, every thing in $M_{persp\rightarrow ortho}$ is known!

  • What’s next?

    • Do orthographic projection(Mortho) to finish
    • $M_{persp}=M_{ortho}M_{persp\rightarrow ortho}$
